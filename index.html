Climate Change: Invasive Species Impact on our Ecosystems

Invasive species have always been an issue to any ecosystem. As climate change has become a larger concern, its impact on invasive species has increased, causing greater irregular hunting and migrating patterns. In order to protect our ecosystems, we as a society need to prioritize taking progressive action towards preventing climate change.

WildLife

WILDLIFE: Invasive species are among the leading threats to native wildlife and approximately 42% or endangered or threatened species are at risk, due to invasive species. The decline and extinction of native species due to invasive predators can have impacts that cascade throughout entire ecosystems. When it comes to invasive animals, there are many aspects that contribute to why they are so concerning. They not only compete with native animals for food and habitats, they sometimes prey on them as well. Climate change can give invasive species an advantage depending on what food and climate they are best inclined to live in. So they can be better at hunting and gathering than native species and they end up leading native species to a disadvantage. Another aspect of invasive animals is the parasites and diseases that they carry that can spread to humans, plants, or other wildlife.

Human Health and Safety

New diseases have made quite the name for themselves throughout human history. Their introduction has always been nothing short of catastrophic. Non-native, invasive species have been responsible for outbreaks, including: rats, mosquitos, and mussels. Disease transmission that has been directly responsible due to invasive species has resulted in numerous deaths and irreversible damage. All we can do now is encourage taking action towards preventing these Invasive species from ever entering their non-native ecosystems. By preventing climate change, we slow the rate, and discourage these species' migrating patterns from altering. We also lessen our impact on their environmental hunting patterns. HOW DO WE HELP: Introduction of Green energy would greatly benefit the environment and animals inhabiting it. Green energy refers to renewable energy sources that have a minimal impact on the environment and reduce carbon emissions. These sources of energy are sustainable and do not rely on finite resources like fossil fuels, which are not only expensive but also contribute significantly to climate change. Some examples of green energy sources include solar power, wind power, hydropower, geothermal energy, and biomass energy. These sources of energy are often harnessed through the use of advanced technologies such as solar panels, wind turbines, hydroelectric dams, and more.

Call to Action

You ~ It starts with one person at a time. You would think that out of 7 billion people one could never make a difference, but that’s not true. For example, if the average person restricted themselves to only 2,000 watts of power each year it would make a huge difference. The average American uses around 12,000 watts, so just imagine how much power could be saved and how little we would be polluting the Earth with such a minor change. Controlled businesses ~ It would be very beneficial if companies could move toward cleaner energy without sending power plants into retirement. An example of this could be a switch to nuclear power that would boost efficiency and decrease fossil fuel emissions. Manage Cities ~ By the year 2050, cities will contain two-thirds of the people on this planet. Therefore, urban areas account for 76% of CO2 emissions, which is very bad for our environment. Even though the populations are high in these areas, it gives them a chance to fix it before it’s too late. That can be done by improving the aging infrastructure, reducing the pollution, and making the cities more attractive for the residents/businesses. Nations ~ The International Panel on Climate Change warned the public about irreversible impacts unless greenhouse gasses are contained, and fast. They however are the ones in control. The nations can create policies, toughen or weaken standards, and take action. The fate of our climate lies in their hands to gain control of this issue and do something about it. Geoengineering ~ This is a term that basically means creating large scale interventions in the environment to counteract climate change. There is a lot of research that is still needed in order to make this work. However, trying to cool down our Earth that is overheating if you will is more complicated than it might seem. Currently people are looking into more efficient energy sources such as nuclear power to combat the carbon dioxide emissions. A more simple version could start with the removal of carbon dioxide. Creating artificial trees with filters might not be a bad idea.
